Mechanical forces shape living organisms

For the past 60 years, the dominant perspective in biological research has been that genes control the lives of living organisms This view has been very successful, leading to an unprecedented advancement in our understanding of the living world But this view misses an important factor affecting lived of living organisms Physical environment of an organisms, also affects cellular life through mechanical forces; these are the forces that I study. I aim to elucidate the general principles of the role of mechanics in biology by studying simplest living organisms - bacteria, which are oftern considered the hydrogen atom of modern biology In today's talk, I will show you a concrete example of how mechanical forces affect living matter and how living matter adapts to forces. But before we get to that, I want to tell you a little bit about my trajectory, to give you a better sense of where I am coming from.
